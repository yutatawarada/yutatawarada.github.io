Crystal structures at finite temperatures

Self-consistent phonon (SCP) theory

The state-of-the-art structural optimization of DFT cannot be applied to finite-temperature phases of materials because lattice vibrations are neglected. Describing structural phase transitions—common in perovskites, ferroelectrics, and TMDs—has therefore been challenging in computational materials science.

We implement a structural optimization theory at finite temperature that minimizes the temperature-dependent free energy computed by an anharmonic phonon theory (self-consistent phonon, SCP). This enables equilibrium structures and phase stabilities beyond the harmonic approximation.

Using this framework, we reproduced the three-step structural phase transition and the p–T phase diagram of BaTiO3 [1].

  1. R. Masuki, T. Nomoto, R. Arita, T. Tadano, “Ab initio structural optimization at finite temperatures based on anharmonic phonon theory: Application to the structural phase transitions of BaTiO3,” Physical Review B 106, 224104 (2022). PRB 106, 224104 (2022) [arXiv]
